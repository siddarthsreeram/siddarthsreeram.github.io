The Brazilian Amazon and Forest Fires

This program analyses data regarding forest fires in recent years and their impact on the area of the Brazilian Amazon as well as their correlation with atmospheric carbon dioxide levels globally. Since much of the world's wildlife resides in tropical regions such as the Amazon, monitoring and mitigating the degredation of the area is crucial.

The program involves a heatmap which graphs data forest fires' geographic and frequency data, a graph which relates carbon dioxide concentrations to forest fire occurences, a graph which plots the degredation of various Brazilian states' Amazonian land.

Mohs Hardness and Mineral Qualities

This program analyses data pertaining to the Mohs Hardness of various naturally occuring minerals and its relationship with other qualities of the mineral. Mohs Hardness is a quantity used to quantify the hardness of a substance and its value can only be determined through experimentation; finding a relationship between a mineral's hardness and another one of its properties would be greatly beneficial in the development of new materials.

The program includes a correlation matrix that relates 5 mineral qualities to each other and determines their correlation as well, a boxplot that graphs the data of 5 selected mineral structures' Mohs Hardness data, a histogram of the distribution of hardness data amongst the 5 mineral structures, as well as the results of a Kruskal-Wallis test that determined statistical significance amongst the 5 structures' data.

Global Seawater Oxygen-18 Concentrations

This program analyses publicly available data collected by NASA regarding global seawater's trend in Oxygen-18 (an isotope of oxygen) concentration over time. The significance of this work arises from O-18 usage as a tracer in many hydrologic studies and a marker of environmental health.

The program has four main components: a heatmap which graphs numerical concentration data upon a map, a graph which relates salinity to O-18 concentrations, a graph which plots O-18 data versus time for a given location, and a central graph which shows the change in global sewater O-18 concentrations over time.

Exploration of U.S. Presidential Addresses

This program analyzes TV rating data on the State of the Union addresses from 1993 to 2020 and aims to find relationships between the rating of a particular address with qualities of the delivered speech.

The program includes three main components: a graph relating TV rating and speech complexity (reading ease) for a particular speech, a graph relating TV rating and positivity for a particular speech, and a word cloud of commonly used words for each speech.

13 Colonies Interactive Map

This program presents a map of the U.S.'s original 13 colonies, each in a different color. When the user clicks on a certain colony, information about its founding will be presented. The program is intended to educate students on the history of the United States.

Wordle4All

This program, developed during a 24-hour hackathon held by the Academies of Loudoun, aims to allow blind or otherwise visually impaired individuals the chance to play the popular online game "Wordle." Through the use of a speech-to-text and text-to-speech library, the program is able to hear the user's voice and speak the results of the on-screen game. Additionally, the tool Selenium was used to "scrape" the details of the webpage (including the colors and buttons on the page) and prepare them to be read aloud to the user.

This project is currently in-progress, as developments are being made in order to hasten the time in which the user's voice detection takes. Also, this project is currently being ported to a browser extension for Google Chrome to allow for the mass usage and effectiveness of the application.

COVID-19 Diagnosis Simulator

This program simulates the diagnosis of an individual with COVID-19: certain behaviors are presented to the user and the user has to determine whether these qualities are related to COVID-19 contraction risk. The score of the user will be presented after he/she has finished one set of diagnoses. The program is intended to educate young children on the safety measures that can be taken to avoid contraction of the virus.
